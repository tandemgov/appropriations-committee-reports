## Reading the gaps

- **Senate FY2021 & FY2023 are blank by design.** Those were omnibus/CR years in which the Senate committee did not mark up and report the twelve bills individually. The enacted stage carries those years instead (1,306 and 1,516 lines), so the two stages are complementary.
- **Enacted FY2025 is a genuine gap.** FY2025 was resolved by a full-year continuing resolution, which produced no new joint explanatory statement on GovInfo to parse.
- **House committee is the full decade — 143/143 of the catalog universe, all re-cleaned on `gemini-3.1-pro-preview` (2026-07-07).** Every House report's hybrid extraction was re-run with the Nemotron first pass reused and the suspect-page Gemini cleanup leg upgraded to `gemini-3.1-pro-preview`, served through **Vertex** (the AI Studio API key's per-minute quota on the preview model is far too low; `GEMINI_VERTEX=1` routes the same model through the GCP endpoint's much higher quota). This supersedes the earlier partial state where 27 backfilled reports were flash-cleaned and one 202-page report (`CRPT-116hrpt9`, Homeland FY2019) was a Nemotron-only baseline — all are now uniformly 3.1-pro-cleaned. The stronger cleanup extracted more rows carrying a checkable amount and made more subtotal blocks reconcile, roughly tripling the absolute `account_inferred` count from ~7,320 to ~19,500 rows (the fraction of value-bearing rows held at ~31%; see below). No wrong data can enter regardless of model: every row is delta-verified independently.
- **Indentation recovery (the non-add double-gate) ran corpus-wide on `gemini-2.5-pro` via Vertex (2026-07-07).** After re-cleaning, all 143 House reports were post-processed by the Gemini non-add double-gate (see [DATA_DICTIONARY.md](DATA_DICTIONARY.md), `is_memo`): for each over-summing subtotal block, Gemini flags the indented non-add sub-details and the block is labeled **only when excluding exactly those lines makes it reconcile**. It recovered ~900 additional blocks (~2,500 rows) that the plain arithmetic reconciler could not, and flagged 3,974 rows as non-add. (The `is_memo` field also folds in **parenthesized non-add memos** — limitations, transfer authorities, GWOT/"of which" breakouts written `(X)` — bringing it to ~8,484 rows corpus-wide; filter these out with subtotals before summing per account.) All 143 reports are marked done (the two heaviest — `CRPT-119hrpt667` at 31 over-sum pages and `CRPT-117hrpt394` — take ~30 min each of Vertex latency; `recover_indent --all-house --write --resume` is idempotent and finishes any report whose pass was interrupted).
- **House committee verified fraction is lower (52.4%) than Senate (92.7%).** This is a property of the source, not a defect: House comparative tables are scanned images extracted by vision, and the "verified" count is the value-bearing rows whose table arithmetic closes exactly. The remainder are structural/label rows (account/agency/section headings, subtotals) and rows that don't express both delta identities — they carry no independently-checkable amount. Zero rows that carry a verifiable amount are silently wrong. The two single-column reports below carry no delta columns at all, so they contribute rows but no delta-verified count, which pulls the House fraction down.
- **`verified` understates corroboration — the `verification_tier` field is the fuller picture.** `verified` is the strict test (a row's *own* delta arithmetic closes). But two other in-document witnesses corroborate amounts that don't pass that specific test, and the `verification_tier` column records them (see [DATA_DICTIONARY.md](DATA_DICTIONARY.md)): `block` — a value-bearing member of a subtotal block whose amounts reconcile exactly (the block sum is a second witness; this is the same evidentiary standard as `account_inferred`), and `inline` — the amount and account restated in the report's string-verified inline funding tables. For House committee rows the tiers are **`delta` 35,832 (52.4%) · `block` 5,616 (8.2%) · `inline` 491 (0.7%) · `none` 26,411 (38.6%)** — so **~61% is corroborated from within the documents**, not 52%. The remaining ~39% (`none`) is structural/label rows plus value-bearing rows whose amount appears nowhere else in the corpus; those are the true boundary of in-document verification, provable only against an external source (USASpending, the committees' own published tables) or by human review.
- **FY2026 and FY2027 are the 119th Congress (added 2026-07-02).** FY2026 (1st session): House 12/12 subcommittees, Senate 8/8 (Labor-HHS srpt55 recovered from its born-digital PDF, see below). FY2027 (2nd session): House 12/12 — the Senate had not yet marked up FY2027 bills as of this writing, so re-run `approps discover` to pick up Senate FY2027 as it publishes. Discovery was extended from the 118th to the 119th Congress; three classifier fixes went with it (the House-renamed "National Security, Department of State" subcommittee, a plural "APPROPRIATIONS BILLS" fiscal-year title, and the line-break-hyphenation fix below).
- **Two House reports carry a single-column statement, not a comparative statement:** Interior-Environment FY2026 (`CRPT-119hrpt215`, 651 rows) and Agriculture FY2027 (`CRPT-119hrpt632`, 303 rows). These bills publish only a "Statement of New Budget (Obligational) Authority — Amounts Recommended in the Bill" (the committee's recommended amount per line, with no prior-year/request/delta columns). Their rows populate `committee_recommendation` only and are self-identifying by the four empty comparison columns; they cannot be delta-verified because there is nothing to reconcile against.
- **Defense FY2027 (`CRPT-119hrpt715`) is born-digital typeset text, not scanned images** — its comparative statement is parsed from the PDF text layer (2,441 rows, 99.7% delta-verified), while its 12 embedded scanned pages are roll-call votes/charts. This supersedes the earlier stand-in (the House Defense full-committee **print**), which remains as a standalone deliverable in `data/output/fy2027-house-defense/` and is no longer counted in the combined dataset to avoid double-counting the same bill.
- **Senate Labor-HHS FY2026 (`CRPT-119srpt55`) — recovered 2026-07-03 from its born-digital PDF (146 inline funding tables).** GovInfo published no HTML rendering for it (a cover-page "refer to PDF" stub); its 451-page born-digital PDF carries funding figures in per-account prose mini-tables (`Appropriations, 2025 … $X` / `Committee recommendation … Y`, whole dollars) rather than a tabular comparative statement. This is exactly the shape the existing inline-table parser already handles — it was never a new-parser problem, only a routing gap: the Senate extraction path was HTML-only. `_extract_report` now falls back to the PDF text layer (`extract_inline_tables_from_pdf`, pdfplumber) when a Senate report's HTML yields no inline tables. All 146 tables carry both prior-year and committee-recommendation amounts, and every extracted amount appears verbatim in the source text (zero unseen). A handful of ALL-CAPS headers lose their decorative drop-cap first letter in the PDF text layer (`ENSION`→Pension, `FFICE`→Office, `DUCATION`→Education); `_repair_dropcap` restores the known cases.
- **Title-hyphenation discovery bug — fixed 2026-07-03, recovered 3 reports.** GovInfo sometimes splits a word across a line in the title, e.g. `...RELATED AGENCIES AP- PROPRIATIONS BILL, 2027`. The discovery pre-filter tested for the substring "appropriation", which the broken form hides, so it silently dropped real committee reports before the (working) subcommittee/FY classifiers ran. The filter now rejoins line-break hyphenation. This recovered **CRPT-117hrpt392** (Agriculture FY2023, 433 rows), **CRPT-118hrpt584** (THUD FY2025, 284 rows), and **CRPT-119hrpt686** (THUD FY2027, 319 rows). **This corrects a prior claim that "THUD has no standalone FY2025 House report":** it does — `CRPT-118hrpt584` — and the same bug had hidden it, so FY2025 House is **12 of 12**, not 11. (THUD did file in FY2023 and FY2027 too.) Same class of bug as the plural-"BILLS" and subcommittee-rename fixes; all three are regression-tested in `tests/test_report_classifier.py`.

## Catalog audit (2026-07-02)

The GovInfo discovery catalog was audited for fiscal-year and report-type errors:

- **Fiscal-year tagging is clean.** Every catalog entry's tagged FY was cross-checked against its title year; after correcting the one untagged report (`CRPT-115hrpt230`, House Energy-Water → FY2018), zero genuine mismatches remain across the corpus.
- **7 Rules Committee resolutions removed.** "PROVIDING FOR FURTHER CONSIDERATION OF THE BILL ..." resolutions (5 about NDAA authorizations, 2 about appropriations bills) had slipped into the catalog because the existing filter only matched the exact phrase "providing for consideration" — not the "further" variant. They contributed **0 data rows** (correctly, as they contain no comparative statement), so the dataset was already clean; they only inflated the catalog and wasted extraction effort. The filter is now robust (`_is_appropriations_report_title`, regression-tested) and the 7 were dropped (catalog 221 → 214).

## Account-hierarchy recovery (2026-07-02)

House vision rows carry no extracted `account` (the label lands in `line_item_text`). The `account_inferred` column recovers the account grouping from the table's own structure: a run of line items closed by a `Subtotal,/Total, <name>` row is labeled with that name — **only when the block's amounts sum exactly to the subtotal**, so every assignment is arithmetic-verified, not guessed. The reconciler is nested (a reconciled inner subtotal collapses into a single addend for its parent, so leaf rows get their account-level name — e.g. DHS "Office of the Chief Financial Officer" resolves to the **Operations and Support** account); it reconciles on any value column, tolerating one OCR-mangled figure; and it recognizes rollup rows the vision model mis-emitted — `Total.` with a period, or a total the `is_subtotal` flag missed — which would otherwise be summed as data and double-count their block. It also excludes **parenthesized memo amounts** from the block sum: in House statements parentheses on an amount (e.g. `(62,596)`) mark a non-add component — a limitation, a transfer, or a gross figure already counted in a net — not a negative, so a block whose total is net-of-memo reconciles once they are excluded (the with-memo sum is tried first, so no already-reconciling block regresses). This labels ~19,500 House rows (≈31% of value-bearing House line items) — the base arithmetic reconciler plus the Gemini non-add double-gate that recovers over-summing blocks (~900 more blocks / ~2,500 rows; see the indent-recovery note above). The absolute count roughly tripled from the pre-re-clean ~7,320: the 3.1-pro re-clean extracted far more rows that carry a checkable amount *and* made more subtotal blocks reconcile, so numerator and denominator grew together and the ~31% fraction held. Additive and arithmetic-verified throughout — no row's amount is changed, and `account_key` crosswalk coverage rises with it, no regression.

## The account-recovery ceiling (investigated 2026-07-02)

Remaining House rows without an `account`/`account_inferred` were investigated in depth to see how much further the recovery could safely go. Six avenues were tried; two are safe and shipped, four were rejected or shelved with evidence:

- **Rollup-variant recognition — shipped.** Recognizing `Total.`-with-a-period rows and totals the `is_subtotal` flag missed (which were being summed as data and double-counting their block) added ~150 correctly-labeled rows.
- **Parenthesized-memo exclusion — shipped.** Excluding parenthesized amounts (`(62,596)` = a non-add limitation/transfer/gross memo, per the House convention) from the block sum, gated on exact reconciliation, recovered ~60 blocks / ~600 rows. This is a formatting-driven rule (the parentheses in the source), distinct from the rejected marker-word approach below.
- **Re-OCR — rejected.** The non-reconciling blocks are **not** OCR errors: the dollar amounts are read correctly. Example — Agriculture "Total, Agricultural Marketing Service" reconciles to the exact dollar once a line-wrap-fragmented duplicate parent-total row and a non-add "transfer from Section 32" line are excluded. Re-extraction returns the identical amounts, so re-OCR cannot help.
- **Section-heading inheritance — rejected (unsafe).** Attributing leaves to the nearest ALL-CAPS heading conflates hierarchy levels: it labels distinct accounts (e.g. "Food Safety and Inspection Service") with a coarse division heading ("DIVISION N"), injecting wrong data.
- **Non-add-marker exclusion — rejected (marginal + risky).** Excluding transfer/limitation-marked lines fixed only ~5 blocks corpus-wide and risks dropping genuine add lines (a "Permanent, (Section 32)" line is an add, not a memo), so the markers don't reliably separate add from non-add.
- **Indentation recovery — prototyped, validated, but not worth scaling (follow-up).** These tables encode hierarchy by *indentation*, and a line indented deeper than its siblings is a subordinate sub-detail (non-add). The vision extraction flattened this — `hierarchy_depth` is unreliable (the AMS transfer line, visibly one level deeper, was recorded at the same depth as its siblings). A prototype re-extracted pages with an indent-aware vision prompt and confirmed the signal is recoverable and *does* reconcile the AMS block to the exact dollar via an indent-depth threshold. But across an 18-page / 40-block sample it recovered **1 block** — the AMS case is real but atypical; most failing blocks fail for other reasons (see below). Projected corpus-wide gain ≈ +1 percentage point of House account coverage, in exchange for re-extracting every House comparative page (paid Gemini + DGX) plus a pixel-alignment build. Not a good trade for a secondary label on already-correct amounts. If ever pursued, use deterministic **pixel** indent measurement (stable) rather than the vision model's run-to-run-drifting levels.

## What the uncovered rows actually are (and why "32%" understates usefulness)

Account-grouping coverage (`account`/`account_inferred`) is ~32% of value-bearing House rows, but that narrow field understates usefulness — the **dollar amounts are ~100% extracted and verified**; only the distilled grouping label is at issue. Of the ~15,300 value-bearing House rows without a grouping label:

| Type | ≈ Rows | Should have an account? |
|---|---:|---|
| Program/detail line with a real text label | 7,700 | Carries its own name in `line_item_text` |
| Short name (member/recipient — earmarks / Community Project Funding) | 3,700 | No — no account by nature |
| Header / zero-value row | 1,900 | No — not a line item |
| Parenthesized non-add memo line | 1,900 | No — a component, not an account |

So roughly **half** the uncovered rows *legitimately* have no account (earmarks, headers, non-add memos — filling them would be wrong), and the other half are program lines that already carry an identifying label in `line_item_text` (usable for joins via `line_item_text` + `account_key`, just not distilled into `account`). The residual failing-block pool that any hierarchy fix could reach is ~2,200 blocks / ~10,600 rows, but — as the indentation prototype showed — the recoverable slice of that is small.

**The real bottleneck for materially higher coverage is cross-page blocks** (subtotal blocks whose line items span a page break, so no single-page pass reconciles them), not indentation. That is the better-targeted follow-up if coverage past ~32% is ever needed. The affected rows' amounts are already correct and delta-verified. See [crosswalk_scoping.md](crosswalk_scoping.md).

## Consumer-side validation: labels vs. totals in the public API (2026-07-03)

Building the public "follow the money" API (`src/approps/api/`, a Sankey flow over this dataset) re-validated the recovery ceiling from the consumer side and surfaced one principle worth recording, because it governs how any downstream aggregation of this data must behave.

**Money aggregation must use only the trusted tiers; account labels and account totals are not the same problem.** The flow sums a metric per account, de-duplicated to account grain (max-magnitude row per account within a report, so an account's total row and its program sub-rows don't double-count). It attributes an account using only `account`/`account_key` (extracted) and `account_inferred` (arithmetic-verified). A third, heuristic tier — `account_recovered`, each unlabeled leaf stamped with the name of the nearest-enclosing `Subtotal,/Total, <name>` block *without* requiring the block to reconcile — is computed at load time but is **never summed and never written to the dataset**; it exists only to report coverage.

The reason is structural and worth stating plainly: `account_inferred` already captures every block whose amounts reconcile, so the recovered tier is by definition the *non-reconciling residual* — its dollar amounts are unreliable by construction. Folding recovered dollars into the flow money was tested and rejected: it re-inflated FY2026 House committee from the trusted **$4.03T** to $10.7T (max-abs), $13.5T (leaf sum), or $14.6T (depth-guarded sum) — i.e. straight back toward the double-counted garbage the account grain exists to prevent. So the API keeps flow totals pinned to the reconciling tier and reports recovery separately.

This is the same family of signal the dataset-level **section-heading inheritance** avenue above rejected as unsafe — attributing leaves to a nearby heading conflates hierarchy levels. The difference is scope: the API keeps `account_recovered` in a non-authoritative *presentation* tier (a coverage diagnostic and a viz label), never promoting it into `account`/`account_inferred` or into any sum, so it cannot inject wrong data. It is trustworthy for *which* accounts money reached, not for *how much*.

Concretely, FY2026 House committee flows **$4.03T across 531 named accounts**; of the rows it can't sum, **~1,071 are structurally attributable** to a section by the recovery pass and **~414 are truly unlabeled**. That split matches the composition table above: roughly half the uncovered rows legitimately have no account (earmarks, headers, non-add memos) and half are program lines that already carry their own label. Raising the *trusted* House total is therefore the same work identified above — better subtotal-amount extraction and cross-page reconciliation to promote `recovered` → `inferred` — and this prototype quantifies the prize: ~14,500 value-bearing House rows corpus-wide currently sit in the recovered tier (correct in amount, structurally attributable, but not yet trustworthy in sum), roughly matching the ~16,000 already in the trusted tier. Enforced in `dedupe_to_account_grain` / `account_tier` (`src/approps/api/data.py`).

## How these numbers are computed

```bash
approps output   # regenerate data/output/comparative_statements.csv + inline_funding_tables.csv
```

Then group `comparative_statements.csv` by `(stage, chamber, fiscal_year)`, counting distinct `report_id`, total rows, and rows where `verified == True`. The combined dataset counts each report exactly once — intermediate House vision passes (`<id>_nemotron.json`, `<id>_hybrid.json`) are excluded from the glob.
